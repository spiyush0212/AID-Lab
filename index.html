ABOUT US

Accessibility and Inclusive Design Lab aims at enabling independent living and empowering persons with special needs by making the world more inclusive and accessible. It attempts to address the challenges that hamper independent and easy access to educational resources as well as information that facilitates navigation (both indoor and outdoor). This lab leverages the principles of human-centred design to create convenient and pleasant day-to-day experiences while solving problems particularly for persons with special needs.

We are an interdisciplinary team of researchers, engineers and designers who are passionately working towards research and projects in the domain of assistive technology, inclusive design and accessibility. Our process follows participatory design models and relies on human interaction and feedback at each step of the design thinking process. Right from identifying potential gaps and challenges through user and field research; design and development processes to deployment of potential solutions for user evaluation, our process is human centred.

We are also working on fundamental research on perceptual fundamentals of design which aims at understanding behavioural and cognitive precepts in information acquisition and learning through empirical research.

Projects

StoryBox: Multi-modal Interactive and Independent Storytelling for Children with Visual Impairment
Storytelling enhances children's imagination, creativity, linguistic skills, emotional skills, and moral values. Limited storytelling media for children with blindness and visual impairment confines their access to independent storytelling. This project explores a multi-modal storytelling agent - StoryBox, which facilitates an independent and interactive storytelling experience for children (5-12 years) with blindness and visual impairment. The platform supports audio-based story narration and enables interaction with story elements via sensor-enabled tactile clay figurines. The concept for StoryBox takes inspiration from 'Kaavad Banchana' - an oral storytelling tradition from Rajasthan, India.

Developers: Bhavya Chopra Jain and Ishika Joshi

Tags: Accessible Learning

Exploring the Design of XR Enabled Accessible Learning Experiences
This project will address this gap and try to explore XR elements such as marked audio captioning, 3D sound, contrast, customizable fonts etc. in the design of resources which are engaging, accessible, immersive and interactive for persons with sensory impairments.

Developers: Sambhav Jain and Ishika Joshi

Tags: Accessible Learning

Accessible Bus Information system and app for users with sensory impairments
This project aims to re-design the current bus information system and make it more accessible for all people, including persons with sensory impairments. The main aim of this project is to provide a user-friendly interface for users to find and plan out their bus trip in the fastest and the most convenient way possible.

Developed by Tanupriya Das

Tags: Accessible Learning

Teaching life skills to blind students using spatial sound (audio AR)
Blind students are late to learn about the life skills needed to connect with people and do not get the exposure, especially during remote times. The project aims in catering to the problem through a role-playing game. The players will learn life-skills using only audio (screen-free), and the whole experience will be engaging and physical & hands-on. They interact with the digital audio and based on their responses or action, the story line is moulded - the behaviour is either rewarded or a lesson is taught.

Developed by Kshitij Aggarwal

Tags: Accessible Learning

Making a healthier workstation
Creating a healthier and space efficient workstation environment that caters to one’s physical as well as mental health.
